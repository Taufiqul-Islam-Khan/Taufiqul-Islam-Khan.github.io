About Me

Researcher, Educator & Engineer

I'm Taufiqul Islam Khan — a fully funded M.Sc. Computer Science student at the University of Manitoba, where I work as a Research Assistant at the MAMBA Lab under Professor Shaowei Wang. My research is in software engineering, specifically code review automation and how large language models can be used to build better developer tools. I also teach COMP 3350 (Software Engineering I) as a Sessional Instructor here.

Academically, I've been consistent since school — Golden GPA 5.00 in both S.S.C. (ranked 17th in my district) and H.S.C. (ranked 82nd in Dhaka Board) from Dhaka College, which is one of the most competitive institutions in Bangladesh. I graduated from BRAC University with a B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering, earning a CGPA of 3.9/4.0, making the VC's List and Dean's List multiple times, and receiving a 70% merit-based tuition scholarship throughout my undergrad.

Before coming to Canada, I spent over three years as a Full-Time Lecturer at BRAC University, teaching Software Engineering, Programming Language I, Computer Interfacing, and Robotics. I was also the Course Coordinator for Software Engineering and Introduction to Robotics (Lab), where I designed course materials, set question papers, and mentored hundreds of students. Even before becoming a lecturer, I was a Student Tutor in the same department from 2018 to 2020, helping juniors with programming and coursework.

On the industry side, I worked as a Software Engineer at BJIT Group, building backend services with Spring Boot and 3dSpace integrations for enterprise clients. Before that, at ServiceEngine BPO, I engineered web scraping pipelines, built ML models for data validation (including a license plate detection system), and set up automated data pipelines on AWS. I've always enjoyed the hands-on side of building real systems just as much as the academic side.

What drives me is the overlap between teaching, research, and engineering. I like building tools that actually help developers work better — and I get a lot of satisfaction from helping students figure things out along the way.
